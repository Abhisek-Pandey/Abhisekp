Make in Odisha Conclave 2022: A Journey of Innovation and Opportunity

Attending the Make in Odisha Conclave 2022 from November 30th to December 4th in Bhubaneswar was a transformative experience, blending insights into Odisha's industrial future with personal discoveries and professional growth. The event, organized by the Government of Odisha, aimed to propel the state into a new era of industrial and technological advancement.

A Grand Beginning

The conclave kicked off with a powerful inaugural session led by Chief Minister Naveen Patnaik. The Chief Minister highlighted Odisha's strategic focus on new-age technologies and skilling initiatives, announcing an impressive budgetary provision of Rs. 100 crores for training youth in fields like cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and data sciences. This commitment set the tone for the event, underscoring the state's dedication to harnessing technology for future growth.

Investment Milestones

One of the standout achievements of the conclave was the announcement of investment proposals worth Rs. 10.5 lakh crore (over $130 billion). These investments, expected to create over one million jobs, highlighted Odisha's potential as a major industrial hub. The emphasis was not just on large-scale projects but also on nurturing startups across sectors such as agriculture, food processing, healthcare, and manufacturing.

Immersive Experiences

For me and my other batch mates, attending the conclave was an eye-opening experience. Accompanied by a faculty member, we explored the extensive arrangements made for industry professionals and participants. The diversity of exhibits was astonishing, showcasing everything from advanced jet engines to underwater submarines. These demonstrations provided us with a firsthand look at the cutting-edge technologies driving various industries.

Engaging Discussions

We attended several insightful sessions throughout the event. The first session, focused on building a coherent skill ecosystem in India, emphasized the importance of aligning skill training initiatives with market demands. Moderated by Ms. Usha Padhee, Principal Secretary of the Skill Development & Technical Education Department, the discussion featured a distinguished panel, including representatives from the World Bank, UN Women India, and the Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation.

Another impactful session on industry-led skilling addressed the disruptions caused by Industry 4.0 technologies such as AI and robotics. This session offered intriguing insights into the gig economy, demographic shifts, and the evolving landscape of work in India. Hearing from experts like Ms. Trishaljit Sethi, Director General (Training) of the Directorate General of Training, and Mr. Manoj Agrawal from Maruti Suzuki, provided a deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities ahead.

Personal Reflections and Learning

A memorable part of our participation was the opportunity to present our innovative ideas during a dedicated tour. Engaging with industry experts and receiving feedback on our concepts was invaluable. It was inspiring to see how our ideas were received and to learn from the experiences of others. The exhibition also featured 130+ stalls showcasing the skilling prowess of various institutions and industries. We interacted with representatives from ITI, Polytechnic, engineering colleges, and technical universities, gaining insights into the diverse skilling initiatives underway across Odisha.

Conclusion

The Make in Odisha Conclave 2022 was not just an event but a catalyst for future growth and innovation. The massive investment commitments, focus on technology, and nurturing of startups all point to a bright future for Odisha. For us, it was a journey of learning and inspiration, providing a unique blend of professional insights and personal growth. Odisha's commitment to becoming a hub of industrial and technological excellence was evident throughout the conclave. For businesses and entrepreneurs looking to expand in India, Odisha presents a compelling opportunity with its investment-friendly policies and burgeoning infrastructure. Attending future Make in Odisha Conclaves will undoubtedly offer valuable opportunities for growth and collaboration.
